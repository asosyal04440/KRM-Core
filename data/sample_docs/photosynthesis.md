# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria use light energy to make glucose. In plants it happens mainly in chloroplasts. The usual inputs are carbon dioxide, water, and light. The usual outputs are glucose and oxygen.

Photosynthesis stores energy in chemical bonds. It supports food webs because organisms can later use glucose for growth or energy. Chlorophyll helps capture light, and carbon dioxide from air becomes part of sugar molecules.
