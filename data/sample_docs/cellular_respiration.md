# Cellular Respiration

Cellular respiration is the process cells use to release energy from glucose. In many eukaryotic cells it happens mostly in mitochondria. The common inputs are glucose and oxygen. The common outputs are carbon dioxide, water, and usable energy in ATP.

Photosynthesis and cellular respiration are linked. Photosynthesis stores energy and releases oxygen, while cellular respiration uses glucose and oxygen to release energy and produces carbon dioxide and water.
